Statement of Purpose

Aims and objectives

When people approach later life we feel that they need to be looked after as they have looked after others throughout their lives. They need a home where individuality is emphasized, with staff who have time to give attention to small detail, and where they have the choice of enjoying the company of like minded fellow Service users. The following core values reflect our belief that every resident has a right to maintain their individually, however, we also have a responsibility for their safety. Therefore, these tow considerations with inform every decision which is made concerning the treatment and care of each person in the home.

PRINCIPAL AIMS & OBJECTIVES

Our main objective is to love and care for the residents placed with us, and to give them peace of mind and reassurance, ensuring that they spend the latter part of their lives comfortably and pleasurably.

The principal aims and objectives of St Margarets to which we aspire, are that:

  • All those who live in the Home do so with dignity, having the respect of those who care for and support them.
  • Residents are entitled to live in a manner and in circumstances which correspond as far as possible with what they were accustomed to in their own home.
  • Residents are given the confidence to feel that St Margarets is first and foremost their home for the rest of their life, if they choose to make it so.
  • We provide a caring, safe, happy and healthy environment for all residents, whilst encouraging individual independence, to help residents maintain and develop their own sense of personal worth and quality of life.
  • Residents are not segregated or denied any opportunity by staff or managers, whether physical, spiritual, intellectual, emotional or social.
  • Relatives and friends of residents are encouraged to be involved in the daily life of their loved ones, in decisions made about their care, and in all social and recreational pursuits.
  • Residents, visitors and staff are protected to the best of our ability from abuse, intimidation and bullying by anyone.
  • Staff are encouraged, supported and trained to fulfill their role to the best of their ability; and to enjoy their working life; thereby providing a happy and cheerful environment for the residents.

    • THE PHILOSOPHY

    THE PHILOSOPHY of St Margarets Care Home is to ensure all residents, relatives and friends are entitled to be treated as individuals. To achieve this, the needs of all Service Users will be assessed and individual care plans developed

    A Statement of PRINCIPLES OF CARE

    Residents of St Margarets are entitled not only to professional care, but to holistic care encompassing their emotional, spiritual and psychological requirements. Residential care is tailored to each resident’s individual needs, and is not for convenience of delivery by the staff.

    Every resident is entitled to the same rights they would expect in their own home. Promoting independence and personal choice is vital, even if it involves an element of risk taking; although we do our best to minimize risks, the resident’s personal preferences remain paramount.

  • All staff endeavors to ensure that every resident is treated with dignity and respect, and that privacy is maintained, regardless of physical or mental disability.
  • Residents are known by their preferred form of address, either their forename or title, as they request.
  • Staffs knock and wait for permission before entering a resident’s room.
  • Staffs afford every resident the maximum of privacy and discretion when assisting with their personal and medical needs. Staff may not discuss the private affairs of any resident with a third party, nor offer comment or opinion on any matter relating to their work

    • St Margarets aims to provide its Service Users with a secure, relaxed and homely environment in which their care, well-being and comfort are of prime importance. Our Care staff will strive to preserve and maintain the dignity, individuality, and privacy of all Service Users within a warm and caring atmosphere, and in doing so will be sensitive to the Service Users ever changing needs. Such needs maybe medical or therapeutic (for physical and mental welfare), cultural, psychological, spiritual, emotional and social, and service users are encouraged to participate in the development of their individualized Care Plans in which the involvement of family and friends maybe appropriate and is greatly valued. This will be achieved through program of activities designed to encourage mental alertness, self-esteem, social interaction with other Service Users and with recognition of the following core values of care that are fundamental to the philosophy of our Home:

    PRIVACY: The right of a Service User to be left alone and undisturbed whenever they wish. DIGNITY: To understand the Service User’s needs and treat them with respect. CHOICE: Give a Service User the opportunity to select for themselves from a range of alternative options. RIGHTS: Keep all basic human rights available to the Service User. FULFILMENT: Enable the Service User to realize their own aims and help them to achieve these goals in all aspects of daily living

    Development

    Our residents are offered encouragement to follow their individual interests and develop new ones during their stay at St Margarets Residential Care Home Much support is given by out-side agencies in helping us to improve the quality of life for our residents during their stay at the home. This includes visits by the hairdresser, chiropodist, optician, dentist and representatives of faith beliefs. Residents have access to local GP services providing excellent support and continuity of healthcare. The home provides 24hours residential care. High standards are of the utmost importance. The Manager is committed to training within the home and has developed links with various training organizations for NVQ levels 2 and 3 in care, and statutory training for all staff. St Margarets Care Home has undertaken a program of refurbishment to ensure that it meet Health & Safety act Standards and the standards required for the Care Quality Commission ( C.Q.C) Regular safety checks and tests are made on equipment including emergency lighting; smoke detector devices and staff are instructed on what to do in the event of a fire. Radiators are guarded and thermostatic valves to baths and basins restrict the temperature of water. The day-to-day management of the home has been designed to make the stay of any resident as enjoyable and as comfortable as possible. Strict attention is given to dietary needs and other details to ensure each resident is provided with an environment not too unlike home retaining individuality, dignity and self-esteem. Our residents exercise the freedom to use or not to use the facilities or to participate or not in the activities organized. We hope that by adhering to the principles described, all who stay or work at St Margarets will have a sense of belonging to a caring establishment.
